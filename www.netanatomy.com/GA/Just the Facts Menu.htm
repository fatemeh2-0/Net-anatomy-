"Just the Facts" 

"Just the Facts" are text files of concise anatomical information that in hardcopy form can serve as a convenient study guide in the absence of the imagery of the NetAnatomy website. Clinical Notes are added to the text files to introduce the reader to some of the clinical relevances of the anatomy under study. The "Just the Facts" files are in the Adobe Acrobat file format (PDF) and require the Adobe Acrobat Reader. If you do not have the Acrobat Reader you can now download a free copy of it by clicking on the GetAcrobatReader logo below. A new browser window will open while you get the Reader – just close the window when you are done and you will return to NetAnatomy.

The Abdomen Dissection menu of the Anatomy Lab has twenty (20) topics listed in three categories, i.e. abdominal walls and peritoneum, organs (viscera), and neurovascular. The greater diversity of structure in the abdomen versus the thorax is evident by the greater number of topics in the Abdomen Dissection menu. There are five “Just the Facts” text files listed below for the Abdomen that contain content on all twenty topics. The topics were organized in a sequence that reflects a common cadaveric dissection approach to the abdomen. For example while it may seem logical to put content pertaining to the anterolateral and posterior abdominal walls in the same “Just the Facts” file, a cadaveric dissection of the entire abdomen would begin with the anterolateral wall and end with the posterior wall. The “Just the Facts” content files for the Abdomen were organized to reflect this type of cadaveric dissection sequence.

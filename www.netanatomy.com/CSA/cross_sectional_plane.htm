The Cross-Sectional Plane

The cross-sectional plane (syn. transverse plane) passes through the body at a right angle to the long axis of the body.  A cross-section is also referred to as a transverse section.
  
The cross-sectional plane is particularly relevant to radiology where computed tomography (CT) scans are primarily generated in the cross-sectional plane. A CT scan is positioned for viewing as if the patient were lying on their back and the viewer was positioned at the patient's feet looking superiorly toward the patient's head. From this perspective the anterior surface of the patient is up, the posterior surface is down, the left side of the body is to the viewer's right, and the right side of the patient's body is to the viewer's left. All of the cadaveric and CT images of Cross-Sectional Anatomy are positioned on the screen in this manner.
